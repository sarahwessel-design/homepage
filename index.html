About Me.txt

I’m a designer interested in digital nostalgia, internet culture, and design as a kind of problem-solving playground. I love early web aesthetics, scrapbook style visuals, and the weird, layered feeling of online spaces. My work often mixes retro interfaces with contemporary ideas, creating visuals that feel familiar but a little unexpected.

I enjoy working with code and digital tools because it feels like solving a puzzle—experimenting, breaking things, and putting them back together in new ways. A recent project exploring the environmental impact of music streaming used a Windows 98-inspired interface, which pushed my interest in webcore and how we consume information online.

I aim to make work that’s clear, engaging, and a little playful—something that invites people to pause, explore, and maybe feel a bit nostalgic along the way.
